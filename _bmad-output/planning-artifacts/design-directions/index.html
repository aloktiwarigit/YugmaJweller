A · Temple Heritage Editorial

Magazine-paced, generous whitespace, serif-heavy Devanagari (Tiro) paired with Cormorant Garamond italics. Ornamental flourishes. Roman numerals. Product detail reads like an editorial feature — cream background with terracotta and gold as sparse accents. Feels like a premium jewellery heritage brand's journal.

Tone

Editorial. Heritage. Considered. Like reading a well-made magazine at a jeweller's counter over tea.

Typography

Tiro Devanagari Hindi (serif Hindi) + Cormorant Garamond (italic serif Latin) + Martel (body)

Risk

May feel too precious for Tier-2 customer browsing. Requires excellent product photography to hold up. Slow-paced, not for quick rate-checkers.

How to evaluate each direction

  • Does a 55-year-old Hindi-speaking shop owner find it familiar? (If it feels alien, it's wrong for Rajesh-ji.)
  • Does a 28-year-old wedding buyer find it premium? (Must rival Tanishq-quality perception.)
  • Does a pilgrim tourist find trust signals (HUID, BIS badge) visible without searching?
  • Does Hindi typography feel crafted, not translated?
  • Is the anchor's brand the hero, or is the design the hero? (Design should serve brand, not upstage.)
  • How does it feel on a $100 Android mid-tier device? (Tier-2 network, 4G, smaller screen.)
